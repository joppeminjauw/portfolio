DISC-test

"People with the dominant temper are active. They say what they have to say and often take the initiative. They are direct in their approach and focus their energy on others. They are controlling and focus on doing things and getting results. Their handling is rational and set to controll their surroundings."

The result of my DISC-test was D, dominance. After reading the characteristics and actions of dominant people, I can agree with certain things. Actually I can relate to all four the types. I think the test is a helpfull base to form a good projectgroup, but not to define people in daily life.

Cultural differences

Power distance

Statement:  In countries with a big power distance people are more depending on others with more power. Because of that they will be less independent and will initiatives always come from those with more power.

Research:  "People with less power have to be depending on people with more power; in practice the less powerful are polarised between dependency and contra-dependency".
I also read an example that states that school expects teachers to take all initiatives and students to follow them blindly. Countries with lower power distance expect the reverse. students get encouraged to come up with ideas and proposals themselves.

Source:   Power distance
